# Licensing

DSOaaS is dual-licensed. Which one applies to you depends on how you use it.

## TL;DR

| Your situation | License |
|---|---|
| Personal use | Free (AGPLv3) |
| Organization under $100K/year revenue | Free (AGPLv3) |
| Organization over $100K/year revenue | Commercial required |
| Offering as a hosted/managed service | Commercial required |
| Building a closed-source product with it | Commercial required |
| Rebranding or white-labeling it | Commercial required |

## Open Source License (AGPLv3)

DSOaaS is available under the [GNU Affero General Public License v3.0](./LICENSE).

AGPLv3 is free to use, but it comes with a key condition: if you run a modified version as a networked service, you must make the full source code of your application available to users of that service. For most personal and small-scale uses, this is perfectly fine.

## Commercial License

If AGPLv3's terms don't fit your use case — or if your organization exceeds the free-use thresholds — a commercial license is available. It removes the copyleft obligations and lets you keep your integrations and modifications private.
